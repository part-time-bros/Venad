01

Nadumuttom Suite

The heart of the home

In traditional Kerala architecture, the nadumuttom — the open central courtyard — is where the family gathers, where light enters the home, and where time moves slowly. This suite is built around one.

A skylight floods the original mosaic floors with morning gold. The king bed faces the courtyard so the first thing you see when you wake is open sky. At night, the courtyard becomes a still, private garden lit by brass oil lamps.

  • King Bed
  • Ensuite Bathroom
  • Rain Shower
  • Writing Desk
  • Garden View
  • Up to 2 Guests

02

Backwater Villa

Where the land meets the water

A private cottage sitting directly on the bank of Vembanad Lake. Your verandah hangs three feet above the water. Traditional kettuvallam houseboats drift past at dawn. Herons fish in the shallows below before the rest of the world wakes.

Inside, a freestanding stone bathtub overlooks the backwaters through a floor-to-ceiling window. The bedroom has its own private entrance from the garden — you are entirely separate from the main house, entirely alone with the lake.

  • Direct Waterfront
  • Private Verandah
  • King Bed
  • Stone Bathtub
  • Private Garden Entrance
  • Panoramic Lake View
  • Up to 2 Guests
₹12,000 per night Taxes included  ·  Breakfast included
03

Plantation Room

The slowest mornings in Kerala

Surrounded by thirty-year-old coconut palms and a working spice garden that Rajan has tended since before you were born. The scent of cardamom comes through the window in the early morning before you even open your eyes.

The open-air stone shower opens onto a private garden enclosure. No roof, no walls — just the sky, the trees, and the sound of birds. This is the room for guests who came to Kerala to breathe deeply and slow everything down.

  • Private Spice Garden View
  • Queen Bed
  • Open-Air Stone Shower
  • Private Garden Enclosure
  • Handwoven Coir Mats
  • Natural Ventilation
  • Up to 2 Guests
₹5,500 per night Taxes included  ·  Breakfast included
04

Heritage Loft

One hundred years, undisturbed

These were the original family quarters of Krishnan Varma, built in 1924. The rosewood ceiling beams have not been touched. The antique brass oil lamps still work. The coir mat flooring is the same weave that was laid when the house was built.

The loft mezzanine — reached by a short teak ladder — holds a single daybed and a window that opens directly over the canal. Guests who have stayed here describe it as the most silent place they have ever slept.

  • Original 1924 Rosewood Beams
  • King Bed
  • Mezzanine Reading Loft
  • Antique Brass Fittings
  • Canal View
  • Coir Mat Flooring
  • Up to 2 Guests
₹9,500 per night Taxes included  ·  Breakfast included
