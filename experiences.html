Sunrise Kayak

The backwaters before the world wakes

Set out at first light, when the canal surface is still as glass and the only sound is your paddle and the distant call of a kingfisher. The narrow waterways that branch off Vembanad Lake are inaccessible by houseboat — this is the only way to see them.

Our guide Biju has paddled these canals since he was twelve. He knows where the otters sleep, where the lotus beds bloom in March, and exactly where to stop so the rising sun catches the paddy fields at the right angle.

What’s included
  • Single kayak or tandem — your choice
  • Guided by Biju through the narrow canal network
  • Fresh coconut water on return
  • Best between October and March

Kerala Cooking Class

The recipes that never leave the family

Priya Varma has been cooking on this wood-fired hearth since she was eight years old, watching her mother, who watched her grandmother. The recipes she teaches are not from a book. They are the ones the family eats every day.

You will learn to make fish curry from the morning catch, soft appam from fermented rice batter, and avial — the Kerala vegetable dish that every family makes differently. You eat what you cook on a fresh banana leaf in the courtyard.

What’s included
  • Three dishes: fish curry, appam, avial
  • Handwritten recipe card to take home
  • Meal served on banana leaf in the courtyard
  • Maximum 4 guests per session

Spice Garden Walk

Kerala through its oldest trade

The garden behind the house has been growing spices since before the house was built. Rajan — who has tended it for thirty years and knows every plant by name — will walk you through cardamom, black pepper, turmeric, vanilla, and a dozen things you have used in your kitchen your entire life without ever seeing growing.

Kerala was the origin of the global spice trade. Standing in this garden, that history is not abstract — it is in the soil under your feet and the scent in the morning air.

What’s included
  • Guided walk with Rajan through the full garden
  • Taste fresh spices at source
  • Small spice packet to take home
  • Available every morning — no advance notice needed

Kathakali Evening

A thousand years of storytelling, in your courtyard

Kathakali is one of the oldest classical performance traditions in the world — a language of gesture, colour, and music that tells stories from the Mahabharata and Ramayana. It takes performers years to master the vocabulary of hand movements alone.

We arrange an intimate evening in the nadumuttom courtyard. You watch the makeup being applied — a ritual that takes over an hour — before the performance begins by brass oil lamp light. The artist will introduce each gesture and what it means before performing it.

What’s included
  • Full makeup application to observe — 60 minutes
  • Intimate 45-minute performance
  • Artist introduction and gesture explanation
  • Requires 48 hours advance notice
₹1,500 per person

Houseboat Day Trip

The whole backwater system, yours for a day

A private kettuvallam — the traditional rice-boat of Kerala, built entirely from bamboo and coir rope — for an entire day on Vembanad Lake and the Kuttanad backwaters. No fixed route, no group, no schedule.

Lunch is prepared onboard by the boat's cook — fresh Kerala fish curry, rice, and whatever was at the morning market. Stop at a village, try fishing with the locals, or simply drift through the paddy fields watching the light change. Return to Venad House as the sun sets over the water.

What’s included
  • Private kettuvallam for up to 4 guests
  • Full Kerala lunch prepared onboard
  • Experienced captain and cook
  • Departure and return from Venad House jetty

Ayurveda Session

Kerala medicine, practised for three thousand years

Kerala is the home of authentic Ayurveda — not the spa version, but the real medical tradition that has been practised here for three thousand years. We work with a certified practitioner from a traditional Kerala Ayurveda family.

The session begins with a brief consultation to understand your constitution, followed by an abhyanga full-body massage using warm oils pressed from herbs grown in our garden. It ends with a herbal steam treatment. Most guests sleep for the rest of the afternoon.

What’s included
  • Brief constitution consultation
  • Abhyanga full-body oil massage — 60 minutes
  • Herbal steam treatment
  • Requires 24 hours advance notice
